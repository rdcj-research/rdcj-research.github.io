Unexpected artefacts

Some lines of inquiry do not resolve into papers, frameworks, or formal arguments. Instead, they produce artefacts.

These artefacts are unexpected not because they are accidental, but because they emerge at the limits of sustained investigation, where structure, constraint, and inquiry begin to express themselves materially. They are outcomes of research that could not be fully anticipated at the outset, and could not be reduced to explanatory text without loss.

The artefacts presented here may take the form of musical systems, physical objects, interfaces, devices, or publications. They function neither as illustrations nor as side projects, but as synthesised expressions of symbolic structure: research that has crossed into form.
